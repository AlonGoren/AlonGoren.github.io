About me

My research focuses on epigenomic mechanisms, and merges basic biology, technological innovations and computational analyses. My major interest is in developmental-epigenomic questions,and includes the explorations of chromatin regulatory networks during mouse embryogenesis. In particular, I am interested in understanding in detail how wellin vitro tissue culture model systems represent in vivo states.

I am a member of the leadership of the Broad Technology Labs (BTL) — the department of The Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT for promoting biotechnological innovations. My group’s efforts are focused on establishing and applying high sensitivity and high-throughput techniques for principled studies of genome-wide chromatin organization. The motivation to innovate these methodologies is driven by the complex nature of developmental-epigenomics, such as the minimal number of cells in each embryo, the dynamic cellular fate decisions, and the complex genomic organization of chromatin regulators. Our goal is to leverage these innovations to enable the study of chromatin in uncharted settings.

I firmly believe that communicating and disseminating of knowledge is a major component of science, and I am involved in mentoring students, presentations of webinars (e.g. Chromatin/ChIP-seq), outreach programs and workshops (e.g. 2014-BroadE).
